Indigenous Services Canada Redesign Project

Summary & Roles

This is a redesign project for the Indigenous Services Canada website. Through user testing and iterations a reimagined style guide and High Fidelity Prototype was actualized for a UI redesign. (School Project)

Molly worked on a team of two for the research aspect of the project and split off into a solo position on the design portion:

  • Research & Heuristic Evaluation
  • Card sort & Site mapping
  • UI Redesign & Navigation Redesign

User Insights

“The site is very text heavy which makes it hard to find the right links, especially when they are hidden in the text.”

“There is too much white space and very minimal organization.”

“There is way too many links and too many clicks to get to one form.”

How might we make the navigation of the website easy and accessible?

Prioritization

  • Reduce text, generalize links to reduce clicks
  • Better categorization & Organization
  • Important links should be easier to find
  • Improve the sites hierarchy for better organization

Card Sort

The site original navigation system had many different path and a wide range of categories. The redesign focused on narrow down the categories and combining content the fit together well.

Personal Reflections

This project allowed me to fully dive into navigational problem solving and accessibility. I learned from user research and testing about users pain points with accessing government content and forms. My research allowed me understand how to best facilitate better navigation through dense content.
